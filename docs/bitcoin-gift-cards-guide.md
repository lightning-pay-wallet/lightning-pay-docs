# Bitcoin Gift Cards Guide

Not every merchant accepts Bitcoin or a linked Bitcoin card directly, and not everyone wants to give Bitcoin itself as a gift. Gift cards purchased with Bitcoin bridge both gaps — letting a BTC or stablecoin balance convert into spendable credit at retailers that only accept traditional gift cards or store credit.

## How it works

Lightning Pay Wallet's gift card feature routes purchases through Bitrefill, a widely used crypto-to-gift-card service, letting a user buy retailer gift cards directly from their BTC, USDC, or USDT balance without a separate off-ramp step. The [gift cards overview](https://lightningpay.info/bitcoin-gift-cards) lists current retailer coverage and typical discount/cashback rates.

## Retailer coverage

Coverage spans major retail categories:

- **Amazon** — available in 20+ regions, with close to 0% premium via Bitrefill cashback in many cases.
- **Walmart** — covering groceries, electronics, and fuel purchases; US-only.
- **Uber** — usable for both rides and Uber Eats orders in supported regions.

Coverage and discount rates vary by region and change periodically based on Bitrefill's own retailer agreements, so the in-app catalog at time of purchase is the accurate source rather than any fixed list.

## Why buy a gift card instead of spending BTC directly

A few scenarios where this makes more sense than direct card spending via the [Bitcoin Visa card](https://lightningpay.info/bitcoin-visa-card):

- **The merchant doesn't accept card payments from the linked card network**, or has restrictions that make a gift card the simpler path.
- **Gifting** — sending a retailer gift card is a more conventional and often more welcome gift than transferring raw BTC to someone unfamiliar with self-custody.
- **Budgeting a specific amount** for a specific retailer, rather than leaving an open-ended card balance available for any purchase.

## Tax treatment

Similar to card spending and bill payments, using BTC or a stablecoin balance to buy a gift card is generally treated as a disposal event for tax purposes in most jurisdictions — the gain or loss is calculated against the cost basis of whatever was spent. The [Bitcoin tax assistant](https://lightningpay.info/bitcoin-tax-assistant) logs gift card purchases alongside other spend categories for exactly this reason.

## Gifting Bitcoin itself vs a gift card

For situations where the recipient is comfortable with self-custody, sending BTC or a Lightning payment directly (covered in the [Lightning Network guide](https://lightningpay.info/bitcoin/lightning-network)) is a more direct option than an intermediary gift card, with no Bitrefill fee or retailer restriction involved. The gift card path exists specifically for situations where the recipient either can't or doesn't want to manage a wallet themselves.

## Using stablecoin balances for gift cards

As with other spending features, gift card purchases can draw from a stablecoin balance rather than BTC directly, which removes exposure to BTC price movement between deciding to make a purchase and it settling. The [stablecoins overview](https://lightningpay.info/stablecoins) covers this trade-off for anyone who prefers predictable amounts over holding BTC exposure right up to the moment of a purchase.
